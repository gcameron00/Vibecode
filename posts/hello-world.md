I've decided to start writing down what I'm learning as I explore AI-assisted development — or as people are calling it these days, *vibe coding*.

## What is vibe coding?

Vibe coding is a style of software development where you describe what you want in natural language and let an AI do the heavy lifting. You're steering by intent rather than syntax. Instead of writing every line yourself, you're having a conversation with a model, reviewing what it produces, and guiding it towards a working result.

It sounds simple, but there's real craft to doing it well.

## Why am I writing this?

A few reasons:

- Writing helps me think more clearly about what I'm actually learning
- I suspect the patterns and pitfalls I encounter will be useful to others
- The tooling and techniques are evolving fast — a running log feels more honest than a polished guide written after the fact

## What to expect

I'll be posting about specific things I've built or tried: what worked, what didn't, and what I'd do differently. Probably some thoughts on the tools themselves too.

No fixed schedule. Posts when I have something worth saying.

Let's see where this goes.
